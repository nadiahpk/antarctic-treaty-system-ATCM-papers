Annual Report pursuant to the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty

NORWAY
1998


1. Notification pursuant to Article 17 of the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty

1.1 Notification of the measures taken pursuant to Article 13, paragraphs 1 and 2, of the Protocol

Norway has taken the following measures to ensure compliance with the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty:

    • Adoption of the Regulations Relating to the Protection of the Environment in Antarctica (“Forskrift om vern av miljøet i Antarktis”) laid down by Royal Decree of 5 May 1995, with authority in § 7 of Act no. 3 of 27 February 1930 relating to Bouvetøya, Peter I Øy and Dronning Maud Land.

The Regulations Relating to the Protection of the Environment in Antarctica entered into force on 5 May 1995.  Since then, a considerable number of implementing procedures has been developed.  To facilitate procedures, questionnaires and guidelines have been developed and are under development.  Several questionnaires and guidelines are developed as common procedures for Norway, Sweden and Finland.  Information about the Regulations and any other procedures can be obtained at the following address:

Norwegian Polar Institute
Polar Environmental Centre
9005 Tromsø
Norway


1.2 Contingency plans established in accordance with Article 15 of the Protocol

The process of developing an Oil Spill Contingency Plan has been initiated for the Norwegian research station Troll and field station Tor.  The plan is expected to be finalized in 1999.

As far as ships are concerned, Shipboard Pollution Emergency Plans (SOPEPs) are required for all vessels utilized by Norwegian Antarctic Research Expeditions.

5.2 Summary descriptions of activities conducted in ASMA or ASPA (Annex V, Article 10 (3) of the Protocol)

Permit No. 1. Summary of bird research activity permitted in SSSI No. 23: 
A group of 4 persons spent 2 months at the field station Tor located in the immediate vicinity of the SSSI to conduct bird research/monitoring in the protected area.  All in all 696 seabirds (Antarctic petrel, Snow petrel and South Polar skua) were banded (catch and release) and 12 Antarctic petrel equipped with satellite senders (catch and release) for further monitoring.  Weighing and measuring of eggs and birds in the colonies were carried out regularly throughout the period.  All activity was conducted in accordance with the management plan for the area.

5.3 Measures taken to implement the provisions of Annex V (Annex V, Article 10 (4) of the Protocol)

Norway has ratified Annex V to the Environmental Protocol (Recommendation XVI-10) and has implemented its provisions through Regulations Relating to the Protection of the Environment in Antarctica which came into force on 5 May 1995.

During the period 1 July 1997 to 30 June 1998 Norway has not conducted any site inspections in any protected area.
