MEMORANDUM FOR THE CHAIRMAN

EIGHTH CONSULTATIVE MEETING OF ANTARCTIC TREATY REPRESENTATIVES

Sir:

Pursuant to your instructions of 9 June 1975 an informal working
group of legal experts met with Dr. Tore Gjelsvik, President of the
Scientific Committee for Antarctic Research (SCAR), to advise him
with respect to certain legal matters arising from SCAR concern with
the need to reserve certain inshore marine areas for scientific
purposes. Representatives from all Consultative Parties were present.

This working group considered the statement by SCAR,

"A number of SCAR National Committees have expressed interest
in the possibility of designating inshore marine areas either
as Specially Protected Areas or as Sites of Special Scientific
Interest. However, at present this is not possible under the
Agreed Measures for the Preservation of Flora and Fauna as
they apply to terrestrial and ice shelf areas only"

The Working Group reviewed both Article VI of the Antarctic Treaty
and Article I of the text of the Agreed Measures for the Conservation
of Antarctic Fauna and Flora published under Recommendation III -
VIII of the Third Consultative Meeting of Antarctic Treaty Repre-
sentatives.

The representatives in the Working Group agreed that the interpre-
tation by SCAR of Article VI of the Treaty and Article I of the
Agreed Measures was too restrictive and that consideration of Marine
areas as Specially Protected Areas or Sites of Special Scientific
Interest is possible under the provisions of the Treaty and the
Agreed Measures for the Conservation of Antarctic Fauna and Flora.

 
                                  James E. Heg
Alternate Representative of
The United States
Working Group Chairman

10 June 1975
