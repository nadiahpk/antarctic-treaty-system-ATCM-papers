SITES OF SPECIAL SCIENTIFIC INTEREST
PLACES OF PALEONTOLOGICAL INTEREST
( SUBMITTED BY CHILE )

The attached report contains an account of the principal sites
of scientific interest that have been discovered in the field of
paleontology, concerning both fauna and flora fossil.

The discovery of these sites is of great importance in connec-
tion with regional Antarctic geological studies carried out by
foreign and Chilean research groups, as they provide an excellent
means of comparison with the rock formations and their fauna which
exist in the southernmost limits of the other continents of the
southern hemisphere. On the basis of this evidence it has been found
that close links exist between the Antarctic Peninsula and the
islands off South America - in particular Tierra del Fuego and
Patagonia - and that there were connections with New Zealand, South
Africa, Madagascar and even India at different geological periods.

For these reasons, and because the fossil deposits are generally
to be found only in limited areas, so that constant searching for
them could result in their total disappearance, it is proposed that
these sites should be designated as sites of special scientific
interest.

The attached map of the Antarctic Peninsula shows the places
referred to in the text, with corresponding reference numbers. We
have also indicated the principal authors who have produced useful
geological and/or paleontological material on the areas with which
we are concerned; a list of their works appears under the heading
"References".

DESIGNATION OF SPECIALLY PROTECTED
AREAS NOS.10,12 AND 16 AS SITES OF
SPECIAL SCIENTIFIC INTEREST

On the basis of the information already submitted, Chile
considers that specially protected areas nos. 10,12 and 16 should
be designated Sites of Special Scientific Interest.

10. Byers Peninsula . Ever since the discovery by Chilean
geologists in 1964 of an important stratigraphical sequence abound-
ing in fauna and flora of the jurassic cretacean age, the study of
which is essential for stratigraphic correlation of the region and
for interpreting the geological evolution of the Antarctic Peninsula.
There has been intensive surveying of the locality. Numerous geolog-
ists of different Antarctic nationalities have been carrying out
studies since then and Chile has organized scientific paleonlogical
programmes which should continue,develop. That is the reason why
this specially protected area should be designated a Site of Special
Scientific Interest.

12. Fildes Peninsula. This specially protected area has been
completely changed by the presence of Bellingshauen and President
Frei Bases. For that,and in view of the following considerations,
this specially protected area should be designated a Site of Special
Scientific Interest: (a) The importance of the presence of rich
deposits of flora and ichnolite fossils of tertiary era (oligocene)
and the need to preserve them from being used as quarries for
- 2 -

building material (Chile has carried out paleontological studies 
which should be pursued); (b) The presence of soft-water lakes on 
this Peninsula whose fauna have been transformed by the existence 
of the bases already referred to. Chile is conducting studies on 
the degree of contamination caused-by-man in the Antarctic environ-
ment.

16. Coppermine Peninsula. In this specially protected area a 
detailed geological study is being conducted. Chile has set up a 
small centre to maintain the continuity of biological research. For 
that reason it is considered that the area should be designated a 
Site of Special Scientific Interest.
