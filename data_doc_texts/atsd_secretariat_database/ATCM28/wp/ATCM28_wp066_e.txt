Measure XXX (2005)
Antarctic Protected Area System: Management Plans for Antarctic Specially Protected Areas

The Representatives,
Recalling Articles 3, 5 and 6 of Annex V to the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty, providing for the designation of Antarctic Specially Protected Areas and approval of Management Plans for those Areas;

Recalling 
    • Recommendation IV-1 (1966), which designated Taylor Rookery as Specially Protected Area No. 1;
    • Recommendation IV-2 (1966), which designated Rookery Islands as Specially Protected Area No. 2;
    • Recommendation IV-3 (1966), which designated Ardery Island and Odbert Island as Specially Protected Area No. 3;
    • Recommendation IV-11 (1966), which designated Cape Shirreff as Specially Protected Area No. 11;
    • Recommendation XIII-8 (1985), which designated Potter Peninsula as Site of Special Scientific Interest No. 13, Harmony Point as Site of Special Scientific Interest No. 14, and Shores of Port Foster as Site of Special Scientific Interest No. 21;
    • Recommendation XIV-5 (1987), which designated Port Foster as Site of Special Scientific Interest No. 27;
    • Recommendation XV-7 (1989), which redesignated SPA 11 (Cape Shirreff) as Site of Special Scientific Interest No. 32; 
    • Recommendation XVI-9 (1991), which designated Forlidas Pond and Davis Valley Ponds as Specially Protected Area No. 23;
    • Measure 3 (1995), which designated Pointe-Geologie as Specially Protected Area No. 24;
    • Measure 2 (1997), which designated the Cape Evans site containing Historical Monuments 16 and 17 as Specially Protected Area No. 25;
    • Measure 1 (1998), which designated the Cape Royds site, containing Historical Monument 15 as Specially Protected Area No. 27, Hut Point, containing Historical Monument 18 as Specially Protected Area No. 28, and Cape Adare, containing Historical Monument No. 22 as Specially Protected Area No. 29;
    • Recommendation XVII-2 (1992) and Measure 3 (1997) which contained revised descriptions and  management plans for SPA 1, 2 and 3, SSSI 13 and SSSI 14;
    • Resolution 7 (1995), Resolution 3 (1996) and Measure 2 (2000) which extended the dates of expiry of SSSI 13, 14, 21 and 32;
    • Decision 1 (2002) which renamed and renumbered these Areas and Sites as Antarctic Specially Protected Areas;

Noting that the Committee for Environmental Protection has advised that two areas, namely Dakshin Gangotri Glacier, Dronning Maud Land and Scullin and Murray Monoliths, Mac. Robertson Land be designated as a new Antarctic Specially Protected Areas, and has endorsed the Management Plans appended to this Measure;
Recognising that these areas support outstanding scientific, wilderness, ecological, heritage and aesthetic values, and would benefit from special protection;
Desiring to adopt Management Plans for these areas, and to replace the Management Plans for Antarctic Specially Protected Areas No. 101, 102, 103, 119, 120, 132, 133, 140, 1451, 149, 155, 157, 158, and 159 with revised and updated Management Plans;

Recommend to their Governments the following Measure for approval in accordance with paragraph 1 of Article 6 of Annex V to the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty:

That:
1. the following be designated as Antarctic Specially Protected Areas:
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 163: Dakshin Gangotri Glacier, Dronning Maud Land;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 164: Scullin and Murray Monoliths, Mac. Robertson Land, East Antarctica;

2. the Management Plans for the following Areas, which are annexed to this Measure, be adopted:
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 101: Taylor Rookery, Mac. Robertson Land, East Antarctica;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 102: Rookery Islands, Mac. Robertson Land, East Antarctica;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 103: Ardery Island and Odbert Island, Budd Coast, Wilkes Land, East Antarctica;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 119: Davis Valley and Forlidas Pond, Dufek Massif;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 132: Potter Peninsula;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 133: Harmony Point;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 140: Sites of Unique Botanical Importance, Deception Island;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 145: Port Foster, Deception Island;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 149: Cape Shirreff and San Telmo Island, Livingston Island, South Shetland Islands;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 155: Cape Evans, Ross Island;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 157: Backdoor Bay, Cape Royds, Ross Island;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 158: Hut Point, Ross Island;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 159: Cape Adare;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 163: Dakshin Gangotri Glacier, Dronning Maud Land;
- Antarctic Specially Protected Area No. 164: Scullin and Murray Monoliths, Mac. Robertson Land, East Antarctica;

3. any prior management plans for Antarctic Specially Protected Areas No. 101, 102, 103, 119, 120, 132, 133, 140, 145, 149, 155, 157, 158, and 159 shall cease to be effective.
