DRAFT RECOMMENDATION SUBMITTED BY NEW ZEALAND AND THE UNITED STATES

AGENDA ITEM 6

November 11, 1966

Specially Protected Areas:  Cape Hallett, Victoria Land

The Representatives, considering that Cape Hallett includes 
a small patch of particularly rich and diverse vegetation which 
supports a variety of terrestrial fauna and that the ecosystem, 
which includes a rich avian fauna, is of outstanding scientific 
interest, recommend to their Governments that the following be 
inserted in Annex B, Specially Protected Areas, of the Agreed 
Measures for the Conservation of Antarctic Fauna and Flora:-

SPECIALLY PROTECTED AREA N° ...

CAPE HALLET, VICTORIA LAND

Lat. 72°18'S., Long. 170°19'E.

DESCRIPTION:  The Area between the eastern side of the 
road, which runs along the eastern side of Willett Cove, 
and the western margin of the permanent ice sheet,  to 
the south of a line from the road to the margin of the 
permanent ice sheet at the latitude of the head of Wil-
lett Cove, and to the north of a line from the road to 
the margin of the permanent ice sheet drawn 350 meters 
to the south of that latitude and parallel to it.  The 
Area is shown on the attached map.
