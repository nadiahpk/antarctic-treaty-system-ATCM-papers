DRAFT RECOMMENDATION SUBMITTED BY JAPAN, AUSTRALIA AND NEW ZEALAND
AGENDA ITEM 4:  MEETING ON LOGISTICS

7 November 1966

The Representatives recommend that their Governments accept the offer made by the Government of Japan to hold a meeting on logistics in Japan in June 1968, and that the terms of reference for such a meeting should be:

1.      The meeting will be held in furtherance of the principles and objectives of the Antarctic Treaty.

2.      The meeting will be a symposium at which experts in selected fields will

      (i)     discuss problems in the fields of Antarctic logistics specified hereunder

      (ii)    exchange views on various possible solutions which have been tried recently

      (iii)   critically examine new solutions which may be proposed in papers submitted at the symposium

      (iv)    work out cooperative arrangements which may be required at the technical or operational level.

3.      The fields for discussion at the meeting might-will include aspects of:

      (i)     Designs of buildings and building services, including waste disposal and water supply

      (ii)    Oversnow transport

      (iii)   Air transport, including airfields

      (iv)    Sea transport
