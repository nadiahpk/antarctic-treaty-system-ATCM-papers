DRAFT RECOMMENDATION SUBMITTED BY THE WORKING PARTY ON AGENDA
ITEM 4 (LOGISTICS)
8 November 1966

The Representatives recommend that their Governments accept
the offer made by the Government of Japan to hold a meeting on
logistics in Japan in June 1968, for a period of about one week,
and that the terms of reference for such a meeting should be:

1. - The meeting will be held in furtherance of the principles and
objectives of the Antarctic Treaty.

2. - The meeting will be attended by experts in selected fields
who will.

     (i)     Discuss problems in the fields of Antarctic logis-
             tics specified hereunder.

     (ii)    Exchange views on various possible solutions which
             have been tried recently.

     (iii)   Critically examine new solutions which may be pro-
             posed in papers submitted at the meeting.

3. - The fields for discussion at the meeting will include as-
pects of:

     (i)     Design of buildings and building services, including
             waste disposal and water supply.

     (ii)    Oversnow tranport.

     (iii)   Air transport, including airfields.

     (iv)    Sea transport.

     (v)     Safety measures.

( vi ) New and urgent problems which Governments agree
require discussion.

4.-  A final agenda within this framework will be determined by
Governments through diplomatic channels after they have had
an opportunity to discuss priorities with their experts.

5.-  The finally agreed agenda should be circulated by the host
Government through diplomatic channels six months prior to
the opening of the meeting.

6.-  A summary of each paper to be discussed at the meeting
should be submitted to the host Government and to other
Consultative Governments through diplomatic channels at
least three months prior to the meeting.

7.-  A report on the deliberations at the meeting will be pre-
pared by the host Government and circulated (for informa-
tion) to all Parties adhering to the Treaty.
