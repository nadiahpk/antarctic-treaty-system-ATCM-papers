DRAFT RECOMMENDATION SUBMITTED BY JAPAN AND AUSTRALIA
AGENDA ITEM 4:  MEETING ON LOGISTICS

7 NOVEMBER 1966

The Representatives recommend that their Governments accept
the offer made by the Government of Japan to hold a meeting on
logistics in Japan in June 1968, and that the terms of reference
for such a meeting should be:

1.  The meeting will be held in furtherance of the principles
and objectives of the Antarctic Treaty as expressed in
Article IX, but Recommendations under Article IX will not
be required.

2.  The meeting will be a symposium at which experts in selected
fields will

    (i) discuss problems in the fields of Antarctic logistics
specified hereunder

    (ii) exchange views on various possible solutions which
have been tried recently

    (iii) critically examine new solutions which may be proposed
in papers submitted at the symposium

    (iv) work out cooperative arrangements which may be required
at the technical or operational level.

3.  The fields for discussion at the meeting will be

    (i) Designs of buildings and building services.

    (ii) Oversnow transport

    (iii) Air transport

    (iv) The problems of water supply at Antarctic stations

    (v) Safety measures
