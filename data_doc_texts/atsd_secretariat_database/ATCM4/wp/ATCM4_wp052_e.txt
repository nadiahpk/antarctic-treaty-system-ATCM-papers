DRAFT RECOMMENDATION SUBMITTED BY NEW ZEALAND
AND THE UNITED STATES

AGENDA ITEM 6

November 11, 1966

Specially Protected Ar as: Beaufort Island, Ross Sea

The Representatives, considering that Beaufort Island
contains a substantial, varied avifauna, that it is one of the
most important breeding grounds in the region, and that it
should be protected to preserve the natural ecological system
as a reference area, recommend to their Governments that the
following be inserted in Annex B, Specially Protected Areas,
of the Agreed Measures for the Conservation of Antarctic Fauna
and Flora.

SPECIALLY PROTECTED AREA N°.....

BEAUFORT ISLAND, ROSS SEA.

Lat. 76°58' S., Long. 167°03' E.

DESCRIPTION: Beaufort Island measures 6
kilometers by 3 kilometers and is located
20 nautical miles north of Ross Island.
The Area is shown on the attached map.
