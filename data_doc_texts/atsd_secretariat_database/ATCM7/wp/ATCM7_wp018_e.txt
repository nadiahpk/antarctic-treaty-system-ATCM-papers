HISTORIC MONUMENTS

(New Zealand National Report on Recommendation
VI-14)

New Zealand has recently made five notices,each notice
consisting of four bronze plaques, one each in Russian,
Spanish, French and English.  These notices will be erected
in October/November 1972 at the following sites:

Scott's Hut, Cape Evans
Shackleton's Hut, Cape Royds
Scott's Hut, Hut Point
Vince's Cross, Hut Point
Scott's Memorial Cross, Observation Hill.

Similar notices will be erected at the remaining
New Zealand proposed historic sites whenever opportunity
permits.

A typical plaque reads as follows:

SCOTT'S HUT, CAPE EVANS

THIS HUT IS AN HISTORIC MONUMENT AND PRESERVED IN ACCORDANCE
WITH PROVISIONS OF THE ANTARCTIC TREATY.  IT COMMEMORATES
THE BRITISH ANTARCTIC EXPEDITION OF 1910-1913 LED BY
CAPTAIN R.F. SCOTT, WHO BUILT THE HUT IN JANUARY 1911.
