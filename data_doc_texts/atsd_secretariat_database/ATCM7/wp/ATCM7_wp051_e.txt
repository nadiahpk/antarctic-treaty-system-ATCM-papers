EFFECTS ON THE ANTARCTIC ENVIRONMENT
AND ON SCIENTIFIC PROGRAMMES OF
INCREASING EASE OF ACCESS
(Draft Recommendation submitted by Belgium,
Chile, France, New Zealand, Norway, South
Africa and United Kingdom.)

EFFECTS OF TOURISTS AND OTHER NON-GOVERNMENTAL
EXPEDITIONS TO THE ANTARCTIC TREATY AREA

The Representatives,

Noting the increasing number of visitors to the Antarctic
Treaty Area who are not sponsored by the Consultative Parties;

Recalling Recommendations VI-7 and VI-11 concerning the
possible harmful effects of such visitors on scientific programmes
and on the Antarctic environment;

Mindful of the need to avoid unnecessary interference over
wide areas with natural ecological systems which are not
sufficiently understood and continue to be the subject of research;

Noting that the Treaty Area contains many unique features of
historical, scenic and general scientific interest;

Recommend to their Governments that:

1.  They keep under review the effects of such visitors
to the Treaty Area in the light of existing
Recommendations;

2.  They consider drawing up at the Eighth Consultative
Meeting a statement of those Principles of the
Antarctic Treaty region about which all visitors
to the Treaty Area should be aware;

3.  They consider designating at the Eighth Consultative
Meeting an adequate number of areas of special

encouraged to go, with a view to avoiding
