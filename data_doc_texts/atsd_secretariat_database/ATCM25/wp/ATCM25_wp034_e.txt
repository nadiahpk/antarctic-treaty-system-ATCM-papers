Antarctic Treaty Inspections


In order to promote the objectives and ensure the observance of the provisions of the Antarctic Treaty, Article VII provides the right to Contracting Parties to designate observers to carry out inspections of stations, installations and other facilities.

In addition, Article 14 of the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty provides that inspections shall be arranged in order to promote the protection of the Antarctic environment and dependent and associated ecosystems, and to ensure compliance with the Protocol.  It also provides that after reports have been circulated to the Parties and to the Committee for Environmental Protection, and have been considered at an ATCM, the reports of such inspections will be made publicly available.

In order to facilitate the planing of inspections, and to assist in keeping records of completed inspections, Australia has compiled the attached list of previous inspections from 1963 to 2001.  This list updates the very useful summary produced in Annex H of the report of the XXI ATCM.

Australia proposes that the attached list be reproduced in the report of this meeting, and that in future the list be kept up to date by the Secretariat for publication, as appropriate.  Australia suggests that any errors detected in the list be notified to the Secretariat and that the Secretariat also keep a list of where copies of the inspection reports, and Parties responses to the reports, may be obtained.
