Update of the activities of the Argentine Antarctic Program on Bioprospection and Bioremediation in the Antarctica

Considering the Resolution 7 (2005) and pursuant to Article  III paragraph (1) of the Antarctic Treaty during the XXIX RCTA in Edinburgh,  Argentina presented and informative paper (IP 112) about the bioprospection and bioremediation activities being performed in the Antarctica and its future activities. On that occasion, an update of the tasks that had been carried out from that time under the framework of the ¨Genoma Blanco¨ (White Genoma) Institutional Project approved by the Instituto Antártico Argentino was presented, together with a list of the scientific publications related to these activities. 
One of the achievements attained was the complete sequenciation of the genome of a new species of Antarctic bacteria. This was not only the first one undertaken with a microorganism of that region, but also the first complete sequenciation of the genome of a microorganism undertaken by the Argentine Republic.  The publication of the scientific work and the deposit of the complete genomic sequence in an international data bank ensure the total compliance with the Article III paragraph (1) of the Antarctic Treaty. (Bizionia argentinensis sp. nov., isolated from surface marine waters, Antarctica. Bercovich A.,  Vazquez S.C., Yankilevich P., Coria S.H., Foti M., Hernández E., Vidal A., Ruberto L., Melo C., Criscuolo M., Marenssi S., Memoli M., Arguelles M., Mac Cormack W.P. International Journal of Systematic and Evolutionary Bacteriology (ISSN: 1466-5026) 58, 2363-2367. 2008).

Another task carried out in our country, which implies the utilization of the metabolic activities of Antarctic microorganisms to obtain an environmental benefit, are the studies on bioremediation of hydrocarbons in soils close to the basis.  Traditionally, the elimination of oil and its byproducts from the contaminated environment has been carried out through technologies that involve physical and/or chemical treatments.  At present, these technologies (that only attain the displacement of the problem from one place to another) are only applied in certain cases and as a first emergency action in order to remove high volumes of contaminants. These processes have been replaced or are complemented by biological treatments that do not negatively affect the environment and that besides allow the elimination of fractions of contaminants that cannot be removed by physical methods. 

For the last three years the Microbiology group of the Instituto Antártico Argentino has kept on performing scientific activities that are related to bioprospection. Basically, the activities carried out are the following:

    I) Studies aimed at the development of processes for the brioremediation of alyphatic and aromatic hydrocarbons on the Antarctic soils. Field surveys have been performed through which the efficiency of the autochthonous flora present in contaminated soils and its response to the addition of nutrients (bioestimulation) has been studied. Moreover, strains and bacterial consortiums with a high capability to breakdown different types of hydrocarbons have been isolated from Antarctic soils. These were used to carry out numerous tests to assess if the inoculation of these biologic systems (Bioaugmentation) increases the removal speed of contaminants.
Also studies of molecular biology are carried out in order to define consortia composition. In a near future this will allow to analyze if it is possible to rebuild the consortium from the individual components. Such action will make enormously easier the preparation of an inoculant reproducible in huge volumes, as it will be required in those cases where the bioaugmentation is confirmed as the selection strategy. Obviously, all the bioaugmentation tests are being developed with autochthonous microorganisms of the Antarctica.


    II) Development and completion of the first two stages of the “Genoma Blanco” project. In the past years the first stages of this project have been completed.  Firstly, an important screening of Antarctic bacteria of different habitat has been completed. All of them were characterized by partial sequenciation of the ARNr 16S gene in order to classify them taxonomically. A strain that showed relevant differences with respect to any other species described was selected and deeply analyzed. This lead to the description of the Bizionia argentinensis, a new bacteria species within the Flavobacteriaceae family.
Once its taxonomic classification was confirmed, the complete sequenciation of the B. argentinensis genoma was performed what resulted in 3.29 Mb. It is planed to analyze the genome in the near future. This task could open the gateway to the finding of new capabilities potentially applicable in biotechnological processes.

    III) Diverse studies focused on increasing knowledge about biodiversity in the mycological flora of the Antarctic ecosystems have been carried out.  This work has allowed the description of a gender and various new species.

    IV) The studies focused on the isolation of bacterial strains highly capable to produce and secret active proteolytic enzymes at low temperatures went on. These strains and their secretion products have a wide application in a varied field of cold enzymes.

    V) The studies about the UV radiation effect and its oscillations over the activity of the Antarctic marine bacterial activity went on.  As part of these studies, marine strains have been isolated, with which numerous studies of dose-response under different irradiance conditions were performed.

    VI) Studies in order to investigate the capability of Antarctic bacteria to biotransform nucleotides have been recently started. These components have a vast field of application as antiviral and antitumoral agents and the biotransformations mediated by complete cells can significantly modify their biological activity.
