2. MEETING TIMETABLE

Plenary and Working Group I sessions take place in Room 
A, while Working Group II sessions are held in Room B-1.
Room B-2 is equipped for meetings of Heads of Delegations and 
other informal meetings. Room A, Room B-1 and Room B-2 are 
located on the 2nd floor of KICH.

The XVIIIth ACTM will be opened in Room A at 10:00 a.m. 
on Monday April 11th. Opening Ceremony will take place from 
10:00 to 10:30 in Room A, followed by plenary session. 
Thereafter the proposed daily working hours are from 9:30 to 
12:30. and from 14:30 to 18:00. Coffee breaks are at 11:00 
and 16:00. An informal meeting of the Head of Delegations 
takes place on April 10th, at 18:00 in Room B-2.

Please see ANNEX 1(1)(2) for the timetable and places of 
meetings, and ANNEX 2(2) for the locations of conference 
rooms.

4. REGISTRATION & SECURITY CONTROL

Only registered participants wearing Conference badges
are permitted to enter and to remain in the Conference area
(2nd floor of KICH). Participants are asked to wear their
badges at all times when they are in KICH. To protect the
interest of all participants, security control is strictly
enforced and no one will be allowed to proceed onto the 2nd
floor of KICH without a badge. Only official delegates with
blue badges will be permitted to enter the meeting rooms(Room
A, B-1, and B-2). Lost badges should be reported immediately
to the Security Officer at the Secretariat.

5. LANGUAGES

The working languages of the Meeting are English, French,
Russian and Spanish. Simultaneous interpretation is provided
for Plenary and Working Group I & II sessions.

6. SUBMISSION OF DOCUMENTS

Delegations are invited to submit documents for the
Meeting at their earliest convenience. In doing so they
should indicate whether the document is to be classified as a
working paper or as an information paper.

Working papers are papers with a direct bearing on a
specific agenda item or items and should, for convenience,
bear a reference on the title page to the agenda item(s) in
question. Delegations are requested to submit working papers
in one of the four official working languages. Subsequently
they will be translated into the other working languages by
the Secretariat.

Information papers are papers of general interest. These
are issued by the Secretariat and distributed only in the
language/languages in which they are submitted.

Information Staff at the Secretariat's Information Desk
and Liaison Officer at meeting rooms receive all documents
for translation and distribution, and take and relay messages
on behalf of delegates.

7. DISTRIBUTION OF DOCUMENTS

registration to delegates. Thereafter all documents are

8. SECRETARIAT SERVICES

Secretariat Information Desk opens from 9:00 a.m. to 7:00 
p.m. daily. Delegates are kindly requested to direct all

9. TRANSPORTATION

Free Shuttle Bus Service between Holiday Inn and KICH, 
which takes about 10 minutes, is available to the delegates 
in the morning and evening.  Please see ANNEX 4(1) for Free 
bus Timetable.

    From hotels in Kyoto city to KICH, some public bus 
service can be used. However, subways are rather 
recommendable when you do not have time. Please go to 
Kitaoji Station by City Subway, transfer to City Bus Line 
Kita-No.4 at Kitaoji Bus Terminal, and get off at the bus 
stop Kokuritsu Kaikan mae.
It takes about 20 minutes from Kitaoji Terminal to the bus 
stop. Taxi service is also available from Kitaoji Station. 
Please see ANNEX 4(2) for City Bus Timetables from KICH and 
Annex 4(3) for Kitaoji Bus Terminal Map.

    For individual trips it is possible to use public bus 
service and subways. Please refer to the enclosed 
Sightseeing Map of Kyoto and ask JTB Travel Booth at KICH for 
tourist information.

THE EIGHTEENTH ANTARCTIC TREATY
CONSULTATIVE MEETING

The following tours are offered free of charge for the participants to enjoy in and around Kyoto.
Participants may attend one of two tours on each day, April 16th(Sat.) and April 17th(Sun.).
For application, please fill out the attached Tour Application Form and send it to Japan Travel
Bureau, Inc. (for address, please refer the application form) by mail or fax by March 31, 1994.
Pick-up services for these tours are available from each hotel. Actual pick-up times are shown
on a ticket which will be handed to you at JTB Booth in the Kyoto International Conference Hall
(KICH). The reservation will be made on a first-come, first-served basis as each tour has a
limited capacity.

April 16 (Sat.)      Free of charge

A   GARDEN TOUR      (9:30 - 17:50)   Lunch included

Hotel ----- Nijo Castle ----- Kyoto Imperial Palace ----- Lunch at Restaurant "Shozan"
----- Golden Pavilion ----- Ryoanji Temple ----- Arashiyama ----- Hotel

NIJO CASTLE: built as Kyoto residence of the Tokugawa Shogunate in the 17th Century,
showing the typical style of the warrior class in stark contrast to that of the court.

KYOTO IMPERIAL PALACE: lived in by the Imperial Family until 1868, with highly refined
architecture and peaceful gardens fitting the elegant court life.

GOLDEN PAVILION: of the Zen Sect, with Golden Pavilion and beautiful "Garden for Strolling".

RYOANJI TEMPLE: provided with a typical garden of "dry landscape", constructed around 1450.

ARASHIYAMA: nice spot with all the beauties of nature and art within its limited area.

B   MIYAKO ODORI      (9:30 - 17:30)   Lunch included

Hotel ----- Heian Shrine ----- Shoren-in Temple (Koto Music) ----- Miyako Odori -----
Lunch at Restaurant "Kyorin[?]en" ----- Sanjusangendo Hall ----- Kiyomizu Temple ----- Hotel

HEIAN SHRINE: patterned after the Imperial Palace which was built toward the end of 8th century.

SHOREN-IN TEMPLE: residence of the head abbot of Tendai Sect. You can enjoy a demonstration
of Koto music in the nice landscape garden.

MIYAKO ODORI: which is called Cherry Blossom Dance, is the festival of geisha girls of the Gic[?]

SANJUSANGENDO HALL: constructed in the 13th century, well known for numerous carved wooden images.

KIYOMIZU TEMPLE: dating back to the 8th century, noted for its unique style of architecture.

April 17 (Sun.)      Free of charge

C   MT. HIEI & LAKE BIWA      (9:00 - 17:30)   Lunch included

Hotel ----- Enryakuji Temple ----- Lake Biwa Cruise ----- Lunch at Biwako Hotel ----- Sanzen-in
Temple/Jakko-in Temple ----- Hotel

ENRYAKUJI TEMPLE: located atop Mt. Hiei in a thick grove of Japanese cypress, established
in 788 to protect Kyoto from evil spirits.

LAKE BIWA: the largest fresh-water lake in Japan. You will enjoy a delightful cruise aboard
a large sightseeing vessel.

SANZEN-IN TEMPLE: an old temple of the Tendai sect at Ohara. It originated when the illustrious
priest Saicho built it on Mt. Hiei.

JAKKO-in TEMPLE: This nunnery was built in perfect seclusion. It is famed as the place where
Empress Dowager Kenreimon-in, mother of the infant Emperor Antoku, became a nun in 1185.

-20-
D   RAPIDS SHOOTING & KIMONO SHOW      (8:00 - 17:00)  Lunch included

     Hotel ---- Kameoka -- (Rapids Shooting) -- Arashiyama ---- Tenryuji Temple ---- Lunch at 
     Restaurant "Rantei" ---- Ninnaji Temple ---- Nishijin Textile Center (Kimono Show) ---- Hotel

     RAPIDS SHOOTING: is an exciting 16 kilometer, ninety-minute trip downstream, shooting the 
     Hozu Rapids aboard a flat-bottomed boat from Kameoka to scenic Arashiyama.

     TENRYUJI TEMPLE: is the headquarters of the Tenryuji school of the Rinzai sect.  It was founded 
     in 1339 by the first Ashikaga shogun, Takauji, in memory of Emperor Godaigo.

     NINNAJI TEMPLE: is the headquarters of the Omuro school of the Shingon sect.

     NISHIJIN TEXTILE CENTER: Nishijin textile is well known as of the highest grade.  Enjoy Kimono 
     fashion show and displays of silk fabrics.
