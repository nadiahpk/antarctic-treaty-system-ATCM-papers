Environmental Study of Argentina's Operational Bases
During the 1993/94 Antarctic Campaign
(Item 6a of the Agenda)

As a result of the 1993/94 Antarctic Campaign, the National
Administration of Antarctica and the Argentine Antarctic Institute
decided to carry out an assessment of the state of the environment
in the zone of influence of the scheduled logistic and scientific
activities and to assess the level of training of staff in charge
of ongoing programs and activities.  This exercise will not only
enable us to identify divergences between the activities which are
undertaken and the future standards of protection, but also will
help to determine what measures are likely to be taken to solve
possible problems.

1.    Preliminary Assessment of the Impact of Argentine Activities
on the Antarctic Environment

To this end, a questionnaire was prepared for the purpose of
summarizing the main environmental characteristics in the zone of
influence of each activity as well as those actions which define
each category of activity with regard to the environment (Annex I).
This questionnaire was distributed to those in charge of each
activity for the purpose of having a summary of those scheduled
activities that might have an impact on the environment, and of
those environment features which are particularly sensitive to
man's activities.

In order to analyze the data gathered from this questionnaire,
matrices were prepared to identify the effects on the environment
(Annex II) in which the interaction between said activities and the
environment are taken into account.

1
Finally, matrices pertaining to a given site (base, 
encampments, or the ship's course) were collected and the enclosed 
table was prepared (see Annex III) which shows the data gathered 
for each site. Also included are proposed mitigation measures for 
those activities likely to harm the environment.

Given that the type of information provided by the 
questionnaires makes it impossible to quantify the scope of 
possible impacts, these assessments are intended to be strictly 
preventive in nature. The results were distributed to those in 
charge of bases and encampments as well as the Argentine 
Administration of Antarctica for consideration of the 
implementation of prevention and mitigation measures on each site.

2. Environmental Study on Argentina's Bases in Antarctica

During the last summer campaign in Antarctica, which followed 
the course taken by the Almirante Irizar ice-breaker, the 
environmental conditions in Argentina's main Antarctic bases were 
surveyed.

The methodology consisted in asking those in charge of each 
station to fill out questionnaire number 2 and in completing 
questionnaire number 3 by carrying out an in situ inspection of the 
surroundings of each base. At the same time, videos were taken of 
each of the visited sites, and the area's geographical references 
were plotted on maps with an acceptable scale of no less than 
1:5000. Also included were those environmental featues deemed to 
be significantly worthy of protection and the territorial extent of 
those human activities potentially harmful to the environment.

This inspection was carried out between January 10th and March 
15th, 1994 by a team comprised of two members of the "Group for 
                                                                                  2
terrestrial ecology and the environment" of the Argentine Antarctic 
Institute as well as a geologist from the University of Buenos 
Aires.

3.	The data and information collected during the above-mentioned 
surveying mission is currently being processed and analyzed.
