THE DEVELOPMENT AND IMPLEMENTATION OF ENVIRONMENTAL
HEALTH AND SAFETY MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS IN THE SOUTH
AFRICA NATIONAL ANTARCTIC PROGRAMME

1. As consultative party committed to compliance to the objectives of the Madrid Protocol on
Environmental Protection, implementation of these objectives has proved the present
requirements of the Protocol to be a good base from which environmental management should be
initiated within our national programme. The complexities of sufficiently integrating
environmental management issues into these programmes however, require that a more
comprehensive system be developed and a greater level of integration be achieved between
environmental concerns and all other aspects of the programmes than is required under the present
Protocol.

2. It was found that by integrating environmental and other management concerns into a single
Environmental, Health and Safety Manager en Sys[?]m (EHSMS), an acceptable level of
environmental performance could be achiev ed 1t we[?] al[?] found that for an EHSMS to be
successful, the inclusion of the following four c[?]mp[?]nents wa[?] essential:

      (a) A mission and policy statement indicat[?] ing he[?] re[?]ult[?] 's ove all commitment to the
      improvement of envir[?]nmental perf[?] n[?] ce.

      (b) A plan and schedule to imp[?]ement the policy throughout the programme.

      (c) A schedule for the integration of the Environmental, Health and Safety Management
      System into all facets of the management and operation of the programme.

      (d) An Environmental, Health and Safety Management programme should be
      implemented under the EHSMS and should include the following detailed elements:

            i)      Management objectives, targets and responsibilities;

            ii)     EHS management structures;

            iii)    a waste management plan;

            iv)     contingency plans;

            v)      a component supporting the provision of information (for example publicizing aspects
            of environmental performance, which is recognized as having value for establishing
            credibility in the eyes of interested and affected parties), and education and training
            programmes to improve all participants' understanding of environmental issues.

            vi)     a comprehensive monitoring plan which includes provision for the maintenance
            of extensive records over time (such as an EHS impacts register);

            vii)    an audit plan to enable the measurement of environmental management performance
            against pre-determined audit criteria. (Management review of the performance, and
            reaction thereto, is also essential).

3. All of these elements have be combined to provide a systematic and structured management 
approach to environmental performance, whilst encouraging the development of innovative 
techniques and technologies to minimize the impact of activities on the environment.

4. A number of standards for Management Systems are available internationally which could be 
utilized to prevent duplication of effort and unnecessary work in achieving this end. They include 
the following:

South African Standard         SABS1251:1003
British Standard               BS7750:1992
International Standards        ISO 9000: ISO 9001: ISO 14000: ISO 14001:
                               OSP 14010-12: ISO 14014: ISO 14015: ISO 14031;
                               ISO 14041-44

Certain shortcomings in practice still existed with the present CEE process in that no clear 
direction is given once and CEE has been excepted. It is felt that TEWG might be in a position to 
address some of the concerned on environmental aspects.

5. To facilitate an improved information exchange between all consultative parties, it is requested 
that the Technical and Environmental Working Group investigate possible means and mechanisms 
to affect the following :

    i)      deviations from the final CEE;
   ii)      implementation of EHSMS components
  iii)      general project progress
   iv)      auditing

By implementing the EHSMS within the South African national programme proved to be a 
success in assisting with the monitoring and auditing of the building process of the SANAE IV 
base. It is believed that the above steps will hold particular benefits for all consultative parties with 
regard to the efficient management of environmental aspects within national programmes.
