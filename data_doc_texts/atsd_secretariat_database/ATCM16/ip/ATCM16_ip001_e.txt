INFORMATION FOR DELEGATES

General Information

1. Place of meeting

The XVIth Antarctic Treaty Consultative Meeting will be held in the

        MARITIM
        Hotel and Congress Centre
        Kurt-Georg-Kiesinger-Allee 1
        D - 5300 Bonn 2

        Telephone: 0228 - 8108-0
        Telex: 886413 mabon d
        Telefax: 0228 - 8108-811

from Monday, 7 October to Friday, 18 October 1991.

Plenary sessions will take place in Saal Schumann on the ground floor of the
conference centre. Working groups will be able to meet in Salon Haydn on the 1st
floor. Salon Koch, also on the 1st floor, is equipped for meetings of Heads of
Delegations and other informal meetings (up to 80 persons).

2. Registration

Only registered delegates with conference badges will be permitted to enter and to
remain in the conference area. Delegates may register in the foyer in front of Saal
Schumann on Sunday, 6 October from 2.00 to 6.00p.m. and on Monday, 7 October
from 8.30 a.m. to 12.00 p.m.
Delegates will be issued with badges upon registration.
3. Security

Delegates are asked to wear their badges at all times when they are in the conference centre. In the interest of all delegates, security at the centre will be strictly enforced and no-one will be allowed to proceed beyond security control without a badge. Lost badges should be reported immediately to the Secretariat.

4. List of participants

Upon registering, delegates will receive a provisional list of participants based on registrations. The final list of participants will be distributed at the end of the Consultative Meeting.

5. Meetings

The Consultative Meeting will be opened in Saal Schumann on Monday, 7 October 1991 at 10.00 a.m. Herr Helmut Schäfer, Minister of State at the Federal Foreign Office, will welcome delegates on behalf of the Federal Government.

Otherwise the proposed daily working hours are from 9.30 a.m. to 12.30 p.m., and from 3.00 p.m. to 6.00 p.m. Refreshments will be provided at 11.00 a.m. and 4.30 p.m.

6. Languages

The working languages of the XVIth Consultative Meeting will be English, French, Spanish and Russian. Simultaneous interpretation will be provided in Saal Schumann and Salon Haydn.

7. Submission of documents

Delegations are asked to submit documents for the Consultative Meeting as soon as possible. In doing so they should indicate whether the document is to be classified as a working paper or as an information paper.

Working papers are papers with a direct bearing on a specific agenda item and should, for convenience, bear a reference on the title page to the agenda item(s) in question. Delegations are requested to submit working papers in one of the four official working languages. Subsequently they will be translated into the other working languages by the Secretariat.
Information papers are papers of general interest. These will be issued by the 
Secretariat only in the language in which they are submitted.
Information papers prepared by the Secretariat will be issued in all four languages.

8.    Distribution of documents

Documents will be distributed via the delegation pigeon holes located in the 
conference foyer on the 1st floor, opposite the Information Desk of the Secretariat 
outside Salon Arndt.

When registering, delegations are requested to stipulate on the document requirement 
form how many documents they wish to receive, and in which of the working languages.

9.    Secretariat services

The Secretariat's Information Desk outside Salon Arndt on the 1st floor will be 
manned from 8.00 a.m. to 7.00 p.m. daily. Delegates are kindly requested to direct all 
enquiries and requests through this channel.

Information staff and liaison officers in the meeting rooms will receive all documents 
for translation and distribution, and take and relay messages on behalf of delegates.

A social diary will be kept at the Information Desk and delegations are asked to notify 
the staff of any hospitality that they may wish to offer.

A photocopier will be available for the use of delegates wishing to make small numbers 
of copies. The Information Staff will assist with larger print runs.

10.    Lunch

The Hotel Maritim will provide a reasonably-priced lunch for delegates in the 
Restaurant on the ground floor from 12.30 p.m. daily. Delegates are of course free to 
use the hotel's other restaurants during their usual opening hours.
11. Social events

Unless otherwise specified, all delegates, as well as Embassy staff involved with the conference, are warmly invited to the social events listed in information paper XVI ATCM/INFO/2.

Departure times for buses providing transfer from the Hotel Maritim/Bonn to the social events are given separately in information paper XVI ATCM/INFO/2.

Delegates are asked to inform the Secretariat when registering, or on Tuesday, 8 October at the latest, whether or not they wish to participate in the weekend trip on Sunday, 13 October and attend the concert on 16 October.

12. Tourist information

During the Consultative Meeting a Bonn tourist information stand will be open from 9.00 a.m. to 1.30 p.m. daily in the foyer outside Saal Schumann. Delegates can obtain information about Bonn and its surrounding area, sightseeing tours, places to visit, restaurants, etc.

Access Plan to Conference Centre                  Annex 3

From the airport, the MARITIM is reached
- by bus (airport - Bonn main station (Hauptbahnhof), journey time about 30 minutes); continue by underground (U-Bahn) (cf "underground").
- by taxi along the autobahn and across the southern Rhine bridge (about 26 km). Cost approx. 50.- DM





In Bonn, the MARITIM is reached
- by underground line 66 (direction Königswinter/Oberdollendorf) to Robert-Schumann-Platz (journey time about 10 minutes), from there about 300 meters' walk to the MARITIM; or underground lines 16 and 63 (direction Bad Godesberg/Rheinallée) to Max-Löbner-Straße, from there about 500 meters' walk to the MARITIM.
- by taxi from the Main Station. Cost about 15.-DM. Taxi ranks are to be found at the main exit of the station and at the Quantius-Strasse exit.
