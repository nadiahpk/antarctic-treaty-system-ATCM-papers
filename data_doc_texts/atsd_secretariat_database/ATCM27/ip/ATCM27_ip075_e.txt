





 





ANNUAL REPORT PURSUANT TO THE PROTOCOL ON ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION TO THE ANTARCTIC TREATY













This report has been prepared in compliance with article 17 of the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty. It should be noted that Belgian Antarctic activities during the period 2003-2004 were embedded in other national or international expeditions, thereby using the facilities and logistics of other nations.


    1. NOTIFICATION PURSUANT TO ARTICLE 17 OF THE PROTOCOL ON ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION TO THE ANTARCTIC TREATY
        1.1 Notification of the measures taken pursuant to Article 13, paragraphs 1 and 2 of the Protocol

The Madrid Protocol has been ratified by the Belgian regional parliaments in 1994/1995. The federal parliament has approved this ratification by its law of May 19, 1995.

Draft legislation to transpose the measures of the Madrid protocol into Belgian law has gone through the Council of Ministers in February and is currently being discussed by the Belgian Parliament.

In order to further its role in marine living resources conservation and management at international level, Belgium has taken steps to become full member of the International Whaling Commission in time for its annual meeting in Sorrento in July 2004. 

        1.2 Contingency plans established in accordance with Article 15 of the Protocol

No contingency plans were set up

    2. REPORT PURSUANT TO ANNEX I, ARTICLE 6 (1) OF THE ENVIRONMENTAL PROTOCOL
        2.1 Procedures to consider the environmental impacts of proposed activities in Antarctica / Annex I, article 6 ( 1a)

All activities in Antarctica undertaken were in compliance with the requirements of the regulations on the terms and procedures of implementing the protocol on environmental protection.

        2.2 List of Initial Environmental Evaluations developed in accordance with Article 2 and decisions taken / Annex I, Article 6 (1b)

During the period 2003-2004 no Initial Environmental Evaluations have been prepared

        2.3 Significant information obtained, and any action taken in consequence thereof, from procedures put in place in accordance with Annex 1, Articles 2 ( 2) and 5, of the Protocol ( Annex I, Article 6 ( 1c))

During the period 2003-2004 no significant information has been obtained in accordance with the provisions of Annex 1

    3. REPORT PURSUANT TO ANNEX II, ARTICLE 6 OF THE ENVIRONMENTAL PROTOCOL
        3.1 Number and nature of permits issued under Annex II of the Protocol during the period 1 September 2002 to 1 July 2003 / Annex II, Article 6/2 of the Protocol

During the period 2003 - 2004 no permits have been issued under Annex II

    4. REPORT PURSUANT TO ANNEX III, ARTICLE  9 OF THE ENVIRONMENTAL PROTOCOL
        4.1 Waste Management Plans prepared in accordance with Annex III, Article 8 of the Protocol / Annex III, article 9/1 of the Protocol

The overall waste management strategy of the Belgian Research Program on Antarctica is that all waste, with the exception of grey water, and some human waste generated in field camps, is retrograded out of the Antarctic Treaty Area for proper disposal.

        4.2 Report on implementation of waste management plan/ Annex III, Article 9/1 of the Protocol

All waste, with the exception of grey water is carried away from the Antarctic Treaty Area for proper disposal.

        4.3 Inventory of past activities in accordance with annex III, Article 8/3 of the Protocol / Annex III, Article 9/1 of the Protocol

No inventory of passed activities has been drawn up at present time.
