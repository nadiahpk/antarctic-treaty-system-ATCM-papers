1. NOTIFICATION PURSUANT TO ARTICLE 17 OF THE PROTOCOL ON ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION TO THE ANTARCTIC TREATY

1.1 Notification of the Measures taken pursuant to Article 13, paragraphs 1 and 2 of the Protocol

Sweden has taken the following measures to ensure compliance with the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty:

The Swedish government decided on 12 September 1991 to sign the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty. After acceptance by Parliament, the Government decided to ratify the Protocol 16 December 1993. The instrument of Ratification was deposited with the Government of the USA on 29March 1994.

The Protocol is published in the English and French versions together with the Swedish translation in the official publication International Agreements of Sweden, SÖ 1994:52, together with the information that the Russian and Spanish texts are available in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

A law enacting the Protocol in Sweden, the Antarctica Act, (SFS 1993:1614) entered into force on 1st April 1994. The Antarctica (Swedish Activities) Ordinance (SFS 1994:95), regulating the application of the Act, entered into force at the same time as the law, on 1st April 1994. The Swedish Polar Research Secretariat has published these two pieces of legislation together with a translation into English, and a small pamphlet "Antarctica is unique" with translation in English. It contains information inter alia on rules for visitors, why a permit is needed to go to Antarctica and how to get a permit. It also informs about the Swedish legislation and the role of the Swedish Polar Research Secretariat.


1.2 Contingency plans established in accordance with Article 15 of the Protocol 

An Oil Spill Contingency Plan for the terrestrial aspects of the Swedish Antarctic Research Program was implemented in 1999. The framework for the Oil Spill Contingency Plan has been developed in co-operation with the Finnish and Norwegian Antarctic Programmes. 

An Expedition Contingency Plan is set up that is compelling for all participants in Swedish Antarctic Programmes. The Expedition Contingency Plan is a flexible tool containing emergency instructions that are adjusted to fit the need of the individual research programme.

Information about contingency plans can be obtained from:

Antarctic Environmental Officer
		Swedish Polar Research Secretariat
		P.O. Box 50003, SE – 104 05 Stockholm, Sweden

4. REPORT PURSUANT TO ANNEX III, ARTICLE 9 OF THE ENVIRONMENTAL PROTOCOL

4.1 Waste Management Plans Prepared in Accordance with Annex III, Article 8 of the Protocol (Annex III, Article 9 (1) of the Protocol) 

A waste management handbook for the Swedish research station Swea was adopted for the first time in 1999. The overall waste management strategy of the Swedish Antarctic Research Program is that all waste, with the exception of grey water, and some human waste generated in field camps, is removed from Antarctica.

A copy of the Handbook can be obtained from the address below:

Antarctic Environmental Officer
		Swedish Polar Research Secretariat
		P.O. Box 50003, SE – 104 05 Stockholm, Sweden
