DRAFT RECOMMENDATION

HISTORIC MONUMENTS

(Submitted by the United Kingdom)

The Representatives,

Recalling Recommendation I-9

RECOMMEND to their Governments :

(1)  that a list of historic monuments which should be 
      preserved be drawn up ;

(2)  that they study the Provisional List appended to 
      this Recommendation with a view to its discussion 
      at the next Consultative Meeting ;  and

(3)  that, in the meantime, they do what is feasible 
      to ensure the survival of the listed items as far as 
      possible.
ANNEX to
ANT 31 REV.1

SCHEDULE OF HISTORIC MONUMENTS

1.  Hut built by Douglas Mawson, January 1912 at Cape Denison
(lat. 67°02'E., long. 142°43'E.)

2.  Hut built by Ernest Shackleton, February 1908 at Cape Royds
Ross Island (lat. 77°33'S., long. 166°07'E.) Restored
in January 1961 by Antarctic Division of New Zealand
Department of Scientific and Industrial Research.

3.  Hut built by Captain Robert Falcon Scott, January 1911
at Cape Evans, Ross Island (lat. 77°38'S., long. 166°24'E.)
Restored 12 January 1961 by Antarctic Division of New Zealand
Department of Scientific and Industrial Research.

4.  Memorial Cross to three members of the Ross Sea Party of
Ernest Shackleton's Trans-Antarctic Expedition, 1914-1916,
who died in the vicinity in 1916. Wind Vine Hill, Cape
Evans, Ross Island (lat. 77°38'S., long. 166°24'E.)

5.  Hut built by Captain Robert Falcon Scott, February 1902
at Hut Point, Ross Island (lat.77°51'3., long. 166°37'E.)

6.  Memorial cross to T. Vince, a member of the British
Antarctic Expedition 1901-1904, erected in February 1904
at Hut Point, Ross Island (lat. 77°51'S., long. 166°37'E).
Partially restored in 1961.

7.  Our Lady of the Snows Shrine. A small rock cairn and
statue of the Virgin Mary erected on Hut point, Ross Island,
in 1956 in memory of Richard Thomas Williams, U.S.N.
(lat. 77°51'S., long. 166°37'E.)

8.  Memorial cross to Captain Robert Falcon Scott's Party
which perished on the return journey from the South Pole,
March 1912, on Observation Hill, Ross Island (lat.77°51'S.,
long. 166°40'E.)

9.  Memorial to Rear-Admiral Richard Evelyn Byrd, U.S.N.,
a bronze bust on a marble pedestal in McMurdo Station
(lat. 77°51'S., long. 166°40'E.)

10. Hut built by C.E. Borchgrevink in February 1899 at
Cape Adare (lat. 71°17'S., long. 170°15'E.)
There are three huts at Cape Adare : two dating from
Borchgrevink's expedition, and one from Scott's Northern
Party, 1910-1911.  Only the southernmost Borchgrevink hut
survives in a reasonable state of repair.

11.  Grave of Nicolai Hanson, a member of C.E. Borchgrevink's
expedition, 1899-1900, at Cape Adare (lat. 71°17'S.
long. 170°15'E.) This is the first known grave in the Antarctic.

12.  Rock cairn and leaden plaque commemorating wintering of
Second French Antarctic Expedition in 1909 at Megalestris Hill,
Petermann Island (lat. 65°10'S., long. 64°10'W.) Restored 1958.

13.  Whalers Cemetery, near Whalers Cove, Deception Island
(lat. 62°59'S., long. 60°34'W.)

14.  Metal plaque erected by Eduard Dallmann at Potter Cove,
King George Island (lat. 62°13'S., long. 58°42'W.), to
commemorate visit of his German expedition, on 1 March 1874.

15.  Hut occupied by main party of Swedish South Polar Expedition,
February 1902 to November 1903, at Snow Hill Island
(lat. 64°22'S., long. 57°00'W.)

16.  Stone hut occupied by three members of Swedish South Polar
Expedition, January to September 1903, at Hope Bay
(lat. 63°24'S., long. 57°00'W.)

17.  Stone hut occupied by crew of wrecked vessel "Antarctic"
of the Swedish South Polar Expedition, February to October,
1903, at Paulet Island (lat. 63°35'S., long. 55°47'W.)
