REPORT ON THE ACTIVITIES OF MARAMBIO  STATION IN THE RECEPTION 
AND BROADCASTING OF METEOROLOGICAL INFORMATION  IN  ANTARCTICA 

(Presented by the Delegation of Argentina)

1. The Antarctic Meteorological Centre "VICECOMODORO MARAMBIO" (64° 
14' S - 56° 43' W) at present concentrates ground sypnotic data 
(SYNOPS) at 00:00, 03:00, 06:00, 09:00,12:00,15:00,18:00 and 21:00 
GMT from the following Sypnotic Argentine Antarctic ground 
meteorological stations:

    88963   ESPERANZA Station
    88968   ORCADAS Station
    88970   MATIENZO Station (from October to March)
    88971   BROWN Station
    89034   BELGRANO II Station
    89045   BELGRANO III Station
    89055   MARAMBIO Station
    89066   SAN MARTIN Station
    89060   PRIMAVERA Station
    88945   CORbeta URUGUAY (Sub-Antarctic)

2. In addition, the following Altitude Meteorological Stations 
(radiosonde-radiowind) receive altitude data (TEMPS) from 00:00 
and 12:00 GMT:

    88968   ORCADAS Station
    89055   MARAMBIO Station
    88946   CORBETA URUGUAY Station (Sub-Antarctic)

3. Likewise, the Antarctic Meteorological Centre "VICECOMODORO 
MARAMBIO" receives direct basic ground and altitude data from 
other Antarctic meteorological stations such as MOLODEZHNAYA 
Station (USSR) through BELLINGSHAUSEN (USSR), and also from 
PRESIDENTE EDUARDO FREI MONTALVA (Chile) Station. ANNEXES A and 
B.

4. Furthermore, MARAMBIO receives all the meteorological 
information continully broadcast by CRT-BUENOS AIRES (Regional 
Telecommunications Centre), data from the remaining Antarctic 
meteorological stations received by CRT-BUENOS AIRES via 
satellite from CMT-WASHINGTON (World Telecommunication Centre) 
through the World Telecommunication System (WTS) of the World 
Meteorological Organisation (WMO), as well as data from Argentina, 
Chile, Australia, New Zealand, etc.of interest in the preparation 
of forecasts for Argentina.
ANT/XI/INF/2                                  - 2 -


5. These meteorological data (basic and processed) that the 
Antarctic Meteorological Centre "VICECOMODORO MARAMBIO" receives 
by different methods, in addition to the meteorological 
information received directly through the Meteorological Satellite 
Image Receiving Station (APT Station), enable MARAMBIO to 
consolidate, process, and broadcast the basic and processed data 
(forecasts and synoptic charts) which serves to provide support 
to maritime and air navigation, land patrols and special technical 
and scientific activities 24 hours a day, as detailed in the 
meteorological data broadcasting program. ANNEX C.

6. The radioelectrical equipment used at present for the METEO 
radiobroadcasting services by teletype and radiofacsimile and 
for radiolink coverage will be replaced in the course of this 
year by four HARRIS RF 745 sets of 5kw each, which will provide 
a wider range and which are more reliable.

7. It is also expected that the receiving plant and the remote 
transmission plant at the Vicecomodoro Marambio Air Base will 
be completed during this year, as well as the installation of 
the antennae field and the new electric generators which will be 
assembled in the main power plant of the Base. This will guarantee 
the reception and transmission of meteorological data.

8. Another factor in the improvement of telecommunications 
in the Antarctic is the forthcoming automation of the CRT-
BUENOS AIRES. This will make possible a speedier and more reliable 
message transmission and a greater control capacity in real 
time and, consequently, a better quality of processed data 
prepared by Argentina for the Antarctic.

                                                  ANNEX B

DIRECT (POINT TO POINT) EXCHANGE OF METEOROLOGICAL DATA
BETWEEN MARAMBIO AND MOLODEZHNAYA STATIONS
THROUGH BELLINGSHAUSEN STATION

1. According to information furnished by the Argentine
delegation during the preperatory meetings for the Tenth
Consultative Meeting of the Antarctic Treaty held in Washington,
D.C. in June-July 1979 ANT (79) PM RPM/3, regular point to
point exchange of meteorological data between the Marambio
and Molodezhnaya Stations was initiated on 12 March, 1979
through the Bellingshausen Station, this link being maintained
until the present.

2. Marambio Station supplies the following data:

    (1) Ground sypnotic data (SYNOPS) at 00:00, 06:00,12:00,
and 18:00 TMG from the following Ground Sypnotic
Metecrological Stations:

        88946   CORBETA URUGUAY         (Argentina)
        88963   ESPERANZA               (Argentina)
        88968   ORCADAS                 (Argentina)
        88970   MATIENZO (From October 
                 to March)              (Argentina)
        88971   BROWN                   (Argentina)
        89034   BELGRANO II             (Argentina)
        89045   BELGRANO III            (Argentina)
        89055   MARAMBIO                (Argentina)
        89066   SAN MARTIN              (Argentina)
        89060   PRIMAVERA               (Argentina)
        88984   PRAT                    (Chile)
        88986   FREI                    (Chile)
        88988   O'HIGGINS               (Chile)

    (2) Altitudic Data (TEMS) at 00:00 and 12:00 TMG from the
following Altitude Metecrological Stations (Radiosonde-
Radiowind):

        88946   CORBETA URUGUAY         (Argentina)
        88963   ORCADAS                 (Argentina)
        89055   MARAMBIO                (Argentina)

    (3) SAREP messages (Coded interpretation of photographs
transmitted via meteorological satellites).

    (4) Forecasts for air and sea navigation, storm warnings
and summary of weather conditions.

    (5) CLIMAT messages.
