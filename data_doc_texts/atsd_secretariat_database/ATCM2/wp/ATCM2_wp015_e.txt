ARRANGEMENTS FOR RADIO-COMMUNICATIONS

Submitted by the Australian Delegation

The Australian Delegation submits for consideration by Govern-
ments intending to send representatives to the Meeting of experts
in Antarctic radio-communications, the following proposed draft
agenda for that meeting:

1.  General Business. Election of Chairman. Adoption of Agenda,
etc., formal acceptance of responsibility for co-ordination
on Antarctic Radio Communications. Discussion of Australian
paper December 15th, 1961.

2.  Communications requirements. Exchange of data on traffic
studies, equipment lists, aerial layouts and site plans.
Consideration of unsatisfactory aspects of existing network
(including boradcasting).

3.  Traffic handling arrangements. Adoption of co-ordinating
time table for handling gear of urgent traffic. Priorities
in normal and post blackout conditions. Possibility of
rationalising existing network by altering number of direct
links.

    Traffic routing

    (a) Within Treaty area.
    (b) Between Treaty area and outside points.

4.  Engineering. Engineering improvement of communications links.
Radio aid for air navigation.

5.  Other Business.

    Scarch and rescue procedure.
    Assessment of costs involved in procedure recommended.
    Continuing liaison.
    Adoption of final report.
