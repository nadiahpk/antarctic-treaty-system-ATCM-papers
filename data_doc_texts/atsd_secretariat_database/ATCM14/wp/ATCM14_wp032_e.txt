FUNCTIONS WHICH MIGHT BE PERFORMED BY A SMALL PERMANENT
INFRASTRUCTURE

The following are some functions which  might be helpful
for a small permanent infrastructure to perform, subject to
directions given to it from time to time by the Consultative
Parties:

1) To assist the host government in the planning and
preparation of Preparatory, Consultative and Special
Consultative Meetings, including assistance with the
preparation of draft-agendas and the distribution of
documents for consideration by the Consultative Parties.

2) To assist the host government with secretariat services
at Preparatory, Consultative and Special Consultative
Meetings.

3) To assist the host government in preparing the Final
Report of Consultative and Special Consultative Meetings
for publication in the official languages of the Treaty,
and to distribute copies of the Final Report in
accordance with the decisions of the Meeting.

4) To revise and publish the Handbook of the Antarctica
Treaty System.

5) To maintain a central archive for documentary information
about the Antarctic Treaty System.

6) To be available, subject to the direction of the
Consultative Meetings, as a focal point for requests for
information about the Antarctic Treaty System.

7) To act as a channel of communication between Treaty
Parties, if so requested by any Treaty Party.
